Rice or Chips Classifier

In this project, I created a machine learning model that cleverly identifies whether a dish contains rice or chips by analyzing images. Starting with a collection of 99 pictures in MLEnd Yummy Dataset, I first standardized each image to a square size to maintain consistency. To refine the data, I utilized two key functions—get_yellow_component and GMLC_features—which reduced the complexity from 9 to just 3 significant features per image. With this streamlined data, I then employed a Random Forest Classifier to train the model, enabling it to accurately predict the content of the dishes. This model serves as a testament to the potential of machine learning in culinary classification and is a highlight in my portfolio of innovative projects.

Veg & NonVeg Classifier

I present another machine learning project where I constructed a pipeline to classify images of dishes from the MLEnd Yummy Dataset as vegetarian or non-vegetarian. Utilizing the TF-IDF technique from natural language processing, I converted the textual data from dish names and ingredient lists into numerical features. With these features, I trained a Multinomial Naive Bayes model, well-suited for text-based categorization. The outcome is an efficient classifier that predicts the dietary category of a dish based on its visual representation.

The best part was that, along with indulging in various dishes, we, as part of the mini-project, created the dataset MLEnd Yummy Dataset with almost 4000 images, where each student contributed 15-20 images.

Content Based Movie Recommendation System

In this project, I crafted a content-based movie recommender system. The journey began with sourcing data from the TMDB 5000 haggle dataset, followed by meticulous data cleaning and dimensionality reduction to ensure quality and manageability. I transformed complex data structures like dictionaries into lists, focusing on genres, overviews, keywords, the top three actors, and the director for each film. These elements were then merged into a singular 'tags' column. To interpret the relationships between movies, I utilized the bag of words model to create vectors and cosine similarity to measure the distance between them.I encapsulated the entire model into two .pkl files—one for the processed data frame and another for the similarity matrix—paving the way for seamless integration into a web interface.
